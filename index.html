About & Research Interests

Hi there, I'm Maggie Zhang. My journey into data science started with a fascination for human cognition and language, and with how we can teach machines to understand the complexity of both. I am currently a student at UC San Diego, double majoring in Data Science and Cognitive Science with a specialization in Machine Learning, and minoring in Linguistics.

My work and research sit at the intersection of AI/ML and computational linguistics. Whether I'm benchmarking automatic speech recognition models in the lab or studying how top-down language models parse syntax compared to human phonetic processing, I am deeply passionate about finding signal in the noise. I love exploring how machines and humans process the world, and I am always looking for ways to bridge structured algorithms and unstructured human behavior.

Professional & Academic Background

My background spans research in bioinformatics, AI engineering, and industry data science. I thrive in environments where I can build end-to-end ML solutions, from data mining and pipeline modularization to predictive forecasting and high-level business strategy.

In professional settings, I focus on translating complex, fragmented data into scalable models that drive strategic decisions. From architecting AI-driven financial forecast dashboards and pressure-testing global corporate data to engineering machine learning algorithms for renewable energy site selection, I enjoy tackling open-ended problems with real-world impact.
